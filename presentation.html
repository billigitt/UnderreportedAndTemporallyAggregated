Terminology and Notation

  • \(\boldsymbol{w}\): The discrete generation time distribution
  • \(I_t\): The reported incidence at time \(t\)

Time-dependent reproduction numbers

Property \(R_t\) \(R_t^c\)
Central equation \(\frac{I_t}{\sum_{s=1}I_{t-s}w_s}\) \(\frac{\sum_{s=1} I_{t+s}w_s}{\sum_kI_{t+s-k}w_k}\)
Definition Average number of people someone infected at time \(t\) could expect to infect should conditions remain unchanged Average number of people someone infected at time \(t\) can expect to infect
Popular Method Epi-Estim Wallinga-Teunis
Pros Real-time estimate, Prior information incorporated, Analytical No inherent condition
Cons Assumes condition remains the same Retrospective only, No prior

Utility of Reproduction Numbers

Within Epidemiology:

  • Assessing the transmissibility present in a population
  • Assessing the effectiveness of interventions
  • Predicting epidemic trajectory? GREY.

Outside of Epidemiology:

  • Population Dynamics, Information Dissemination
